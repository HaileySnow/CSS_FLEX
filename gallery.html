Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci (1503-1506)

A portrait of Lisa Gherardini, celebrated for her mysterious smile and detailed realism. It’s one of the most iconic pieces in the world, housed in the Louvre, Paris.

The Starry Night – Vincent van Gogh (1889)

A swirling depiction of the night sky, this painting captures Van Gogh's emotional state and his fascination with light and movement.

The Last Supper – Leonardo da Vinci (1495-1498)

A fresco showing Jesus and his disciples at the moment he announces betrayal. Its intricate composition highlights the emotional reactions of each figure.

The Persistence of Memory – Salvador Dalí (1931)

This surrealist painting features melting clocks in a dream-like landscape, symbolizing the fluidity of time.

Girl with a Pearl Earring – Johannes Vermeer (1665)

Often called the "Mona Lisa of the North," this portrait captivates with the subject's gaze and luminous light effects.

Lady with an Ermine – Leonardo da Vinci (1489–1491)

This portrait of Cecilia Gallerani, the mistress of Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan, features an ermine symbolizing purity and elegance.
